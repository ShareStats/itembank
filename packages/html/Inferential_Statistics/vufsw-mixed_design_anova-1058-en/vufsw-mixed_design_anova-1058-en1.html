    Question

    A mixed factorial design can be defined as a design that:


    1. FALSE: has always one between-subjects variable and at least one within-subjects variable.
    2. TRUE: has at least one between-subjects variable and at least one within-subjects variable.
    3. FALSE: has at least two within-subjects variables and no between-subjects variable
    4. FALSE: has at least two between-subjects variables and no within-subjects variable

    Solution

    A mixed design has at least one between variable and at least one within variable. So, there is always at least one between variable and more than one between variable is also possible.
