    Question

    In four different studies a regression analysis with one predictor is conducted. Consider the t-tests of the regression coefficient in the four different regression models below. Which of the four different t-tests has the smallest p-value?

    Model t_test
    I t(29) = -1.3
    II t(29) = 3.3
    III t(299) = 1.3
    IV t(299) = -3.3

    1. FALSE: Model I
    2. FALSE: Model II
    3. FALSE: Model III
    4. TRUE: Model IV

    Solution

    Model IV is correct. In general, larger t-values, regardless of whether itâ€™s a positive or negative value, are accompanied by smaller p-values. The sample size also has an influence. The same t-value (as in models II and IV) has a smaller associated p-value in a larger sample. So, according to this logic, the correct answer is model IV. The t-value and sample size (as shown by the df in the parentheses) are the largest here.
