    Question

    The null hypothesis used in hypothesis testing with the Pearson correlation is:


    1. TRUE: the correlation is 0 in the population
    2. FALSE: the correlation is not equal to 0 in the sample
    3. FALSE: the correlation is not equal to 0 in the population
    4. FALSE: the correlation is 0 in the sample

    Solution

    Hypotheses always revolve around the population, not your sample. And a null hypothesis expects no effect/difference/relation.
