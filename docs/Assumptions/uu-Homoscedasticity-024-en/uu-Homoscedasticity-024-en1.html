    Question

    There is heteroscedasticity if…


    1. FALSE: The explanatory variables are highly correlated among themselves.
    2. FALSE: The measurement errors (‘errors’) are not normally distributed.
    3. FALSE: The relationship between at least 1 of the X variables and the Y variable is nonlinear.
    4. TRUE: The variance of the measurement errors (‘errors’) is not constant across the X variable(s).
