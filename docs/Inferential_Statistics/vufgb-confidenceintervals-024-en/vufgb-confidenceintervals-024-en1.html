    Question

    The mean in a sample of 25 persons is 15, the variance is 9.

    Calculate the 95% confidence interval for the population mean.


    1. FALSE: 13.82 ; 16.18
    2. TRUE: 13.76 ; 16.24
    3. FALSE: 11.28 ; 18.72
    4. FALSE: 14.75 ; 15.25

    Solution

    95%CI:mean±t.025×se95 \% \; CI: \; mean \pm t._{025} \times se

    So, firstly you have to find the corresponding t-value and calculate the standard error.

    t-value at df=n−1=25−1=24:2.064df = n-1 = 25-1 = 24: \; 2.064

    se=sn=35=0.6se = \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{3}{5} = 0.6

    When you fill this in, you get: 15±2.064×0.615 \pm 2.064 \times 0.6, which is 13.76;16.2413.76 \; ; \; 16.24.
