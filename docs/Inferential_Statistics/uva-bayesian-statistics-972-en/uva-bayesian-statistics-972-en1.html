    Question

    Suppose it is known that a proportion of 0.002 of Dutch women have disease D. There is a test to detect this disease. That test is not always reliable: in 0.04 of the cases in which the person has the disease, the test gives the result Òno disease = negative result’. 0.96 of them are detected with a positive result. Furthermore: 0.96 of those who do not have the disease get a negative result. Marja is told that she has a positive test result.

    Set the events:

    • G = No disease D,
    • P = The positive test result.

    Then what is the probability P(G and P) that Marja gets a positive test result and she too has no disease?
