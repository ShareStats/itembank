    Question

    It is known that 18% of the labor force in the Netherlands suffer from back and neck pain. Based on a survey performed among randomly selected students, 46 out of 200 students turned out to suffer from such pain. Derive the p-value and test the null hypothesis that students do not differ from the labor force of The Netherlands. Use a significance level of 5%.


    1. FALSE: p = 0.093, the null hypothesis is not rejected.
    2. TRUE: p = 0.066, the null hypothesis is not rejected.
    3. FALSE: p = 0.047, the null hypothesis is rejected.
    4. FALSE: p = 0.033, the null hypothesis is rejected.
