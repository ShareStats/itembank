    Question

    Below you can find the observed scores on a depression scale with a one‐week interval.High scores indicate high levels of depression.

    Participant Form1 Form2
    1 17 19
    2 21 23
    3 14 11
    4 28 24
    5 32 34
    6 24 21

    Calculate the test‐retest reliability. To decrease your work: s1st2=37.06s^2_{1st} = 37.06, s2nd2=54.56s^2_{2nd} = 54.56 and c1st,2nd=37.89c_{1st,2nd} = 37.89.


    Solution

    The test‐retest reliability is equal to the correlation between the two scores on the two administrations. This correlation is: rF1,F2r_{F1,F2} = 37.8937.06×54.56=.843\frac{37.89}{\sqrt{37.06 \times 54.56}} = .843.
