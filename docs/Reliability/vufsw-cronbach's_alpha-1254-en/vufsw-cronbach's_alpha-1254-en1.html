    Question

    For his thesis research, a graduate student measured the degree of self-confidence of students with a scale consisting of 6 items, whereby the answer categories are ranging from 1 (little self-confidence) to 10 (a lot of self-confidence). The Cronbach’s alpha of this scale is 0.68.

    How could the Cronbach’s alpha be increased?


    1. TRUE: Add an additional item to the scale.
    2. FALSE: Remove an item with a high corrected item-total correlation.
    3. FALSE: Remove an item with a value of 0.65 for “Cronbach’s alpha if Item Deleted”.
    4. FALSE: Recode all the answer categories of items, so that 1 means “a lot of self-confidence” and 10 means “little self-confidence”.

    Solution

    You can increase the Cronbach’s alpha by adding an extra item.
